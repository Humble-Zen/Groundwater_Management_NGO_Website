Groundwater Conservation

Understanding and Protecting Our Underground Water Resources

What is Groundwater?

Groundwater is the water found underground in the cracks and spaces in soil, sand, and rock. It is stored in and moves slowly through geologic formations called aquifers.

Did you know? Groundwater provides drinking water for 51% of the total U.S. population and 99% of the rural population!

Why Conservation Matters

Groundwater is being depleted at alarming rates due to:

  • Over-pumping for agriculture and urban use
  • Pollution from industrial and agricultural activities
  • Climate change affecting recharge rates
  • Population growth increasing demand

Conservation Tips

At Home

  • Fix leaky faucets and pipes
  • Install water-efficient appliances
  • Take shorter showers
  • Collect rainwater for gardens

In the Garden

  • Water plants early morning or evening
  • Use drought-resistant plants
  • Apply mulch to retain moisture
  • Install drip irrigation systems

In the Community

  • Support water conservation policies
  • Report water waste
  • Participate in local cleanup efforts
  • Educate others about conservation

Take Action Now

Every drop counts! Start implementing these conservation practices today to help protect our valuable groundwater resources for future generations.

Remember: Groundwater is a finite resource. Once depleted or contaminated, aquifers can take hundreds or thousands of years to replenish.
